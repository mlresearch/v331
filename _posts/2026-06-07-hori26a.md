abstract: We propose a new approach for solving planning problems with a hierarchical
  structure, fusing reinforcement learning and MPC planning. Our formulation tightly
  and elegantly couples the two planning paradigms. It leverages reinforcement learning
  actions to inform the MPPI sampler, and adaptively aggregates MPPI samples to inform
  the value estimation. The resulting adaptive process leverages further MPPI exploration
  where value estimates are uncertain, and improves training robustness and the overall
  resulting policies. This results in a robust planning approach that can handle complex
  planning problems and easily adapts to different applications, as demonstrated over
  several domains, including race driving, modified Acrobot, and Lunar Lander with
  added obstacles. Our results in these domains show better data efficiency and overall
  performance in terms of both rewards and task success, with up to a 72% increase
  in success rate compared to existing approaches, as well as accelerated convergence
  ($\times 2.1$) compared to non-adaptive sampling.
