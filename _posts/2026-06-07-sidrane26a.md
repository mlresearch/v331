abstract: Learning-enabled planning and control algorithms are increasingly popular,
  but they often lack rigorous guarantees of performance or safety.  Frameworks such
  as reachability analysis can be used to provide such guarantees.  We introduce an
  algorithm for computing underapproximate backward reachable sets of nonlinear discrete
  time neural feedback loops. We then use the backward reachable sets to check goal-reaching
  properties. Our algorithm is based upon ideas from robustness analysis for vision
  networks, and on overapproximating the system dynamics function. Together these
  enable computation of underapproximate backward reachable sets through solutions
  of mixed-integer linear programs. We rigorously analyze the soundness of our algorithm
  and demonstrate it on a numerical example.  Our work expands the class of properties
  that can be verified for learning-enabled systems.
