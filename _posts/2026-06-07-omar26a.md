abstract: 'We present a unified framework for multi-task locomotion and manipulation
  policy learning grounded in a contact-explicit representation. Instead of designing
  different policies for different tasks, our approach unifies the definition of a
  task through a sequence of contact goals–desired contact positions, timings, and
  active end-effectors. This enables leveraging the shared structure across diverse
  contact-rich tasks, leading to a single policy that can perform a wide range of
  tasks. In particular, we train a goal-conditioned reinforcement learning (RL) policy
  to realise given contact plans. We validate our framework on multiple robotic embodiments
  and tasks: a quadruped performing multiple gaits, a humanoid performing multiple
  biped and quadrupedal gaits, and a humanoid executing different bimanual object
  manipulation tasks. Each of these scenarios is controlled by a single policy trained
  to execute different tasks grounded in contacts, demonstrating versatile and robust
  behaviours across morphologically distinct systems. Our results show that explicit
  contact reasoning significantly improves generalisation to unseen scenarios, positioning
  contact-explicit policy learning as a promising foundation for scalable loco-manipulation.
